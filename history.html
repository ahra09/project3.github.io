History

Korean seasonal cuisine embodies a history of adapting to nature's bounty.

The term “Korean” relates to the culture, cuisine, and traditions of South Korea, a country located on the Korean Peninsula in East Asia. South Korea has a rich cultural heritage and a diverse cuisine that has evolved over thousands of years.

Korean cuisine places a strong emphasis on seasonality. Each season brings its own unique ingredients and flavors, and Koreans traditionally believe that eating in harmony with the seasons is essential for maintaining good health.

Lunar Calendar

The Korean Lunar Calendar remains a vital cultural and historical element, preserving traditions and connecting people to their heritage.

The Korean Lunar Calendar, in use for over a millennium, has been pivotal in establishing essential dates for traditional ceremonies, agricultural activities, festivals, and holidays. Rooted in Korean culture and tradition for centuries, this calendar is lunar-based, featuring months that fluctuate in length between 29 and 30 days, distinguishing it from the Gregorian solar calendar.

Activities

Korean activities have a rich history of simple, communal pastimes passed down through generations.

Korean seasonal cultural activities are an integral part of the country's rich cultural heritage, reflecting its agricultural traditions and the importance of the changing seasons. These activities encompass a wide range of festivals, customs, and practices that are celebrated throughout the year.
