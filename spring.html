Spring

[봄]

In spring, Koreans savor a variety of vibrant and fresh greens known as “namul,” blanched and seasoned with garlic, sesame oil, and soy sauce. These seasonal greens bring a burst of color and flavor to the table. Mugwort, a spring herb, is used to create dishes like “ssuk tteok,” a rice cake believed to have health benefits. Dishes featuring fragrant wildflowers and herbs are also popular during this season.

Activities

Korean Traditional Wrestling

[씨름]

Korean traditional wrestling, known as “ssireum,” is a popular sport and cultural tradition in Korea. It involves two competitors grappling and trying to force their opponent to touch the ground with any part of their body other than the feet or to step out of a designated ring. Ssireum has a long history in Korean culture and is often featured in festivals and competitions.

Lantern Festival

[연등축제]

Korean traditional lantern festivals, known as “Yeon Deung Chukje,” are vibrant events celebrated in various cities and towns across South Korea. These festivals feature the display of numerous paper lanterns illuminating the night sky, creating a beautiful and captivating spectacle. They are celebrated for cultural, religious, and festive reasons, attracting locals and tourists alike.
