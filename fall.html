Fall

[가을]

Fall is a season for savoring the abundant harvest. Especially Korean Asian pears, are enjoyed during this season. People also embrace seasonal mushrooms and chestnuts, incorporating them into various dishes, and preparing “songpyeon,” crescent-shaped rice cakes.

Traditional Korean folk dance

[강강술래]

Traditional Korean folk dance is closely associated with Chuseok, the Korean autumn harvest festival. This folk dance is particularly popular during Chuseok celebrations and is often performed by women in rural communities. It is a circle dance where participants join hands and move in a circular formation while singing traditional songs.

Yutnori

[윷놀이]

Yutnori is a traditional Korean board game that is often played during Korean holidays and family gatherings. The game is played with four short sticks called “yut” and one long stick called “mo.” Players take turns throwing the yut sticks, and based on how they land, they can move forward or gain other opportunities. Yutnori is typically played in two teams, and team members work together to try and secure a win. It is considered a part of traditional Korean culture and is enjoyed annually among family and friends.
