Summer

[여름]

Summer in Korea is a season characterized by the desire for cool and refreshing culinary delights. It's a time when people seek solace from the heat in a variety of dishes and snacks that provide a respite from the scorching sun. Traditional foods and contemporary treats emerge, capturing the essence of the season with their flavors and textures. These culinary traditions are a testament to the enduring desire for comfort and refreshment during the Korean summer.

Activities

Washing hair in water with iris leaves

[창포물에 머리 감기]

The washing hair in water with iris leaves, known as 'changpo-mul', has been believed to enhance hair luster and promote hair growth. Throughout history, women have engaged in washing hair in water with iris leaves, considering it a method to maintain the health of their hair. It’s not limited to hair wrapping; there is also a tradition of consuming water in which believed to be beneficial for overall health.

Swinging

[그네타기]

Swinging provided physical activity and entertainment for children, allowing them to enjoy the outdoors, especially in the shade during hot summer days. It was a part of the traditional Korean folk culture during the Joseon Dynasty and promoted healthy play and enjoyment among children, as they swung high into the air with friends and family.
