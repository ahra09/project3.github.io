Samgyetang

Ingredients

- 1 whole young chicken (about 2-3 pounds)

- 2-3 cloves of garlic

- 1 cup of glutinous rice

- 4-5 ginseng roots

- 5-6 dried jujubes

- 4-5 chestnuts

- Salt and pepper to taste

- 10 cups of water

Instructions

1. Clean and rinse the young chicken thoroughly, removing any excess fat and impurities.

2. Soak the glutinous rice in cold water for about 30 minutes, then drain it.

3. Stuff the chicken's cavity with soaked glutinous rice, garlic, ginseng roots, and dried jujubes.

4. Sew the cavity shut with kitchen twine or use toothpicks to secure it.

5. Place the stuffed chicken in a large pot and add water, covering the chicken completely.

6. Bring the pot to a boil, and then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until the chicken is tender and the rice is fully cooked.

7. Add chestnuts to the pot and simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes.

8. Season the samgyetang with salt and pepper to taste.

9. Serve the samgyetang hot in individual bowls, and enjoy the hearty and nourishing Korean ginseng chicken soup!

Note: You can also garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds for added flavor.
