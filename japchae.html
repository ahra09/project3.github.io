Japchae

Ingredients

- 100g sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon)

- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

- 2 cloves garlic, minced

- 1 small onion, sliced

- 1 small carrot, julienned

- 1 red bell pepper, sliced

- 2 cups blanched spinach

- 150g shiitake mushrooms, sliced

- 150g sliced beef

- 2 tablespoons soy sauce

- 1 tablespoon sugar

- 1 tablespoon sesame oil

- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

- Salt and pepper to taste

- Green onions for garnish

Instructions

1. Boil the sweet potato starch noodles (dangmyeon) in boiling water for about 6-7 minutes or until they are fully cooked but still chewy. Drain, rinse with cold water, and set aside.

2. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and sliced onions, and sauté until the onions become translucent. Add the julienned carrots and continue to stir-fry for a few minutes. Add the red bell pepper and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Remove the cooked vegetables from the skillet and set them aside.

3. In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced beef and stir-fry until it's almost cooked. Then, add the shiitake mushrooms and continue to stir-fry until the beef is fully cooked, and the mushrooms are tender.

4. Return the cooked vegetables to the skillet with the beef and mushrooms. Add soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil. Stir to evenly coat all the ingredients. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

5. Add the cooked sweet potato starch noodles to the skillet and stir-fry everything together until well combined. The noodles should take on a slightly translucent appearance.

6. Lastly, add the blanched and drained spinach to the skillet and toss it with the other ingredients until heated through.

7. Remove the skillet from heat, and garnish with toasted sesame seeds and thinly sliced green onions. Serve your homemade Japchae warm or at room temperature.
