Tteokguk

Ingredients

- 1 cup of sliced Korean rice cakes (tteok)

- 6 cups of water

- 5-6 dried anchovies

- 1 piece of dried kelp (about 2x2 inches)

- 1/2 cup of thinly sliced cooked beef or brisket

- 2 eggs

- Salt, to taste

- Thinly sliced green onions, for garnish

Instructions

1. In a large pot, combine 6 cups of water, dried anchovies, and dried kelp. Bring it to a boil, then simmer for about 15-20 minutes to make a flavorful broth.

2. Remove the anchovies and kelp from the broth, and discard them. You should be left with a clear and savory broth.

3. Add the sliced Korean rice cakes (tteok) to the broth and cook for about 10 minutes or until they become soft and tender.

4. Stir in the thinly sliced cooked beef or brisket, and continue to simmer for another 5-10 minutes.

5. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs.

6. Slowly drizzle the beaten eggs into the simmering soup while gently stirring the soup. This will create delicate egg ribbons in the soup.

7. Season the soup with salt to taste.

8. Serve your Tteokguk hot, garnished with thinly sliced green onions.
